About

I am an Assistant Professor in Macroeconomics at the University of Birmingham. My research sits at the intersection of macroeconomics and political economy, with a focus on how individuals form beliefs and expectations and how those beliefs shape economic and political behaviour.

My work has appeared in journals including the Journal of the European Economic Association, the European Economic Review, Economic Theory, and Electoral Studies.

Research interests

  • Belief & Opinion Formation
  • Behavioural Macroeconomics
  • Comparative Politics
